FAQ

TRON Energy Questions

Everything you need to know about TRON Energy, USDT TRC20 fees, renting vs burning TRX, and how TronMax works.

What is TronMax?

TronMax is a non-custodial TRON Energy rental marketplace that helps users reduce USDT TRC20 transaction fees. Instead of burning TRX on every transfer, you rent TRON Energy from TronMax for a fraction of the cost — typically ~3 TRX vs 13–15 TRX burned per USDT transaction. You can also sell unused energy and stake TRX to earn passive rewards.

What is TRON Energy and why do I need it?

TRON Energy is a network resource required to execute smart contract operations on the TRON blockchain — including every USDT TRC20 transfer. A standard USDT transfer consumes approximately 65,000 units of energy. If you don't have enough energy in your wallet, the TRON network automatically burns your TRX to cover the cost, resulting in transaction fees of 13–15 TRX per send.

How does TRON Energy rental work?

When you rent TRON Energy on TronMax, a provider (who has staked TRX and generated surplus energy) delegates that energy directly to your wallet via TRON's native resource delegation mechanism. The energy appears in your wallet within seconds. You then use it to execute USDT TRC20 transfers at zero or near-zero cost during the rental period. When the rental expires, the energy returns to the provider.

How much does it cost to rent TRON Energy on TronMax?

The standard market rate on TronMax is approximately 420 SUN per energy unit. A USDT TRC20 transfer requires 65,000 energy, making the total rental cost around 27 TRX for a 1-day rental — compared to burning 13–15 TRX per single transaction. If you send multiple USDT transfers per day, the savings compound dramatically. Bulk and long-duration rentals are available at rates as low as 350 SUN per unit.

Is TronMax custodial? Do I need to send TRX to TronMax?

TronMax is fully non-custodial. You never deposit TRX into a TronMax account or surrender control of your funds. The rental payment is made directly on-chain from your wallet, and the energy delegation happens via TRON's native protocol. TronMax never holds your private keys, seeds, or any user funds. All interactions are wallet-signed and transparent on the TRON blockchain.

How fast is the energy delegation on TronMax?

Energy delegation on TronMax is near-instant. Once your rental payment is confirmed on the TRON blockchain (typically within 1–3 seconds), TronMax's automated delegation engine transfers the energy to your wallet within 5 seconds in most cases. You can begin sending USDT TRC20 transactions immediately after delegation is confirmed in your wallet.

What is the difference between TRON Energy and Bandwidth?

TRON has two types of network resources: Energy (used for smart contract execution, including USDT TRC20 transfers) and Bandwidth (used for simple TRX transfers and on-chain data storage). USDT TRC20 transactions primarily consume Energy. TronMax supports rental of both Energy and Bandwidth, though Energy rental is the most in-demand product as USDT TRC20 is the dominant use case.

How many USDT TRC20 transfers can I make with rented energy?

Each standard USDT TRC20 transfer consumes approximately 65,000 energy units. So if you rent 650,000 energy, you can execute approximately 10 USDT transfers within the rental period. Contract interactions with more complex logic (like DeFi swaps) may consume more energy. TronMax's calculator widget shows you exactly how many transfers you can make for any energy amount you order.

Can I sell my unused TRON Energy on TronMax?

Yes. If you have TRX staked (frozen) in your wallet, you generate TRON Energy every day. If you don't use all of it yourself, you can list your surplus energy on TronMax's marketplace and earn TRX from buyers who rent it. Energy selling is automated — set your price, TronMax handles the delegation and payments. Payouts are made daily in TRX directly to your wallet.

What APY can I earn by selling energy on TronMax?

Energy sellers on TronMax typically earn approximately 6–10% APY on their staked TRX, depending on current market energy prices and demand. This is calculated as: daily energy generated × current energy rental price per unit × 365 / TRX locked × TRX price. TronMax displays real-time APY estimates based on live market rates in the staking and selling dashboard.

What rental durations are available for TRON Energy?

TronMax offers energy rentals across multiple durations: 1 hour, 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, and 30 days. Longer durations provide better per-unit pricing. For example, renting energy for 7 days is significantly cheaper per day than 7 separate 1-day rentals. Choose the duration based on how many USDT TRC20 transfers you expect to make in that period.

Does TronMax support TronLink wallet?

Yes. TronMax is fully compatible with TronLink (the official TRON browser extension and mobile wallet), as well as other TRON-compatible wallets including TokenPocket, Klever Wallet, imToken, Trust Wallet (TRON network), and any WalletConnect-compatible TRON wallet. Simply connect your wallet on the TronMax website to access all features.

What is TRON Energy staking and how does it work on TronMax?

Staking on TRON (also called "freezing") means locking TRX for a minimum of 14 days in exchange for Energy or Bandwidth resources. TronMax's staking pool aggregates TRX from multiple users, generates a large pooled energy supply, and distributes daily energy rewards proportionally. You can stake any amount of TRX, use your energy allocation for free transfers, or sell it on the marketplace for passive income.

How does TronMax compare to staking TRX myself?

Self-staking TRX requires a minimum lock-up of approximately 1,500 TRX (about $100+) to generate enough daily energy for 1 USDT transfer per day. TronMax's rental model lets active senders access the same energy for just ~3–27 TRX — no capital lock-up required. For HODLers with large TRX positions, TronMax's staking pool offers a way to monetize that staked capital efficiently through energy selling.

Is TronMax safe to use? Has it been audited?

TronMax operates as a non-custodial marketplace using TRON's native delegation mechanism — meaning no smart contract exploits can drain your funds, as no funds are deposited into TronMax contracts. All TRON Energy delegation is handled through TRON's built-in protocol-level resource delegation, not third-party smart contracts. TronMax's platform code and infrastructure are regularly reviewed for security. Always verify you are on the official domain tronmax.vercel.app.

What happens when my energy rental period expires?

When your rental period ends, the delegated energy automatically returns to the energy provider's wallet via TRON's protocol. Your wallet balance and funds are completely unaffected. If you still have unused USDT transfers to make, simply rent a new batch of energy on TronMax. You can also set up auto-renewal for continuous energy coverage without interruption.

Yes. TronMax is actively used by cryptocurrency exchanges, USDT payment processors, OTC desks, and high-volume TRON-based businesses. Custom pricing and SLA agreements are available for enterprise volumes.

Does TronMax support bandwidth rental for TRX transfers?

Yes. In addition to Energy, TronMax also supports Bandwidth rental for users who frequently make simple TRX transfers or on-chain data storage operations. Bandwidth prevents account freeze fees on TRX-to-TRX transfers. The Bandwidth rental market is smaller than Energy, but TronMax provides competitive pricing for both resources through the same unified marketplace interface.
